<p><p>Dental indirect restorative materials are used to restore damaged or missing teeth through an indirect procedure. These materials are fabricated outside the mouth by a dental laboratory, and then cemented onto the teeth by a dentist. They include materials such as dental ceramics, dental alloys, and dental composites.</p><p>The dental indirect restorative materials market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The rising prevalence of dental disorders and the increasing demand for cosmetic dentistry are major factors driving market growth. Additionally, the growing geriatric population and the rising awareness about dental hygiene and aesthetics are further fueling market expansion.</p><p>Technological advancements in the field of dental materials are also contributing to the growth of the market. Novel materials with enhanced properties, such as better strength, durability, and esthetics, are being developed, thereby increasing their adoption in dental restorations.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing demand for minimally invasive procedures and the availability of advanced dental techniques are propelling the market's growth. Dental indirect restorative materials offer a conservative approach to restore teeth while preserving as much of the natural tooth structure as possible.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a rising preference for all-ceramic restorations over metal-ceramic restorations, as they provide superior esthetics. Additionally, the demand for digital dentistry is increasing, with the adoption of computer-aided design/computer-aided manufacturing (CAD/CAM) technology for the fabrication of dental restorations.</p><p>Overall, with the market expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.80% during the forecast period, the dental indirect restorative materials market presents lucrative opportunities for players in the dental industry.</p></p>

<p><p>The dental indirect restorative materials market is categorized into four types: metal-ceramics, ceramics, resins, and others. Metal-ceramics are a combination of metal alloys and ceramic materials, providing strength and aesthetics. Ceramics are tooth-colored materials that offer excellent aesthetics but may lack strength compared to metal-ceramics. Resins are tooth-colored composite materials that provide good aesthetics but may have limited durability. The "others" category includes materials like glass ionomers and hybrid materials that offer a blend of aesthetics and strength, but with varying characteristics.</p></p>
