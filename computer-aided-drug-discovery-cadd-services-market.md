<p><p>The Computer-Aided Drug Discovery (CADD) Services market includes various types of services used in the development of new drugs. Virtual Screening involves analyzing large databases to identify potential drug candidates. Molecular Docking predicts how small molecules interact with target proteins, helping to design effective drugs. Molecular Modeling uses computer simulations to study drug-target interactions and optimize their properties. Scaffold Hopping involves modifying or replacing certain parts of a drug molecule to improve its effectiveness. Lastly, Other services refer to additional techniques and technologies used in drug discovery, such as QSAR (Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationship) modeling and ADMET (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion, and Toxicity) predictions.</p></p>
