<p><p>Residential energy storage batteries are classified into three main types: lead-acid batteries, lithium-ion batteries, and other types. Lead-acid batteries are traditional, reliable, and have a low cost but have limited capacity and may require maintenance. Lithium-ion batteries provide higher energy density, longer lifespan, and faster charging capabilities, but they are more expensive. Lastly, other types include nickel-cadmium, sodium-sulfur, and flow batteries, each with their own unique advantages and limitations. These battery options cater to different residential energy storage needs, offering a range of cost, capacity, and performance characteristics.</p></p>

<p><p>Residential energy storage batteries are utilized in two main applications - grid-tied and off-grid markets. This stored energy can be utilized during high demand periods or when the grid experiences disruptions. In contrast, off-grid market refers to standalone systems where batteries provide a reliable and continuous power supply to residential properties that are not connected to the main power grid, often found in remote areas or during emergencies.</p></p>
