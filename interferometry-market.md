<p><p>Interferometry is a technique used to measure small displacements, variations in refractive index, or surface characteristics. The market for different types of interferometers includes Laser Fizeau Interferometers, Mach-Zehnder Interferometers, Michelson Interferometers, Fabry-Perot Interferometers, Sagnac Interferometers, and Twyman-Green Laser Interferometers. Laser Fizeau Interferometers use a laser source to create an interference pattern, whereas Mach-Zehnder Interferometers split and recombine a beam of light for measurement. Michelson Interferometers use a beam splitter to measure variations in light path length. Fabry-Perot Interferometers analyze the transmission and reflection properties of multiple beams. Sagnac Interferometers measure rotation effects, while Twyman-Green Laser Interferometers are used for optical testing and evaluation purposes.</p></p>
