<p><strong>The Analytics Cloud Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Public Cloud</li><li>Private Cloud</li><li>Hybrid Cloud</li><li>Community Cloud</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Public Cloud: This type of cloud market refers to analytics services and resources provided by third-party vendors over the internet. It is open to anyone and allows for scalability, cost-effectiveness, and flexibility.</p><p>Private Cloud: In this market, analytics services and resources are accessed only by a single organization. It offers enhanced security, control, and customization options. Private clouds are usually hosted on-premises or by a dedicated third-party provider.</p><p>Hybrid Cloud: This market combines the features of both public and private clouds, allowing organizations to use a mix of on-premises and off-premises resources. It offers increased flexibility, scalability, cost optimization, and the ability to leverage existing investments.</p><p>Community Cloud: In this market, analytics services and resources are shared by a specific group of organizations with shared interests, such as government agencies or industry associations. It enables collaboration, cost-sharing, and customization specific to the community's needs.</p></p>
