<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Thin film substrates are materials used in electronic packaging to provide support, insulation, and interconnection for various electronic components. Rigid thin-film substrates are solid and inflexible, commonly made of materials like ceramics or glass. They offer stability and can withstand higher temperatures. On the other hand, flexible thin-film substrates are bendable and typically made of materials like polyimide or polyester. They offer flexibility, allowing for easier integration into curved or flexible electronic devices, but may have lower thermal resistance compared to rigid substrates.</p></p>
