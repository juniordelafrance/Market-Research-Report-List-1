<p><p>Drug delivery across the blood-brain barrier (BBB) refers to the ability to transport therapeutic drugs effectively from the bloodstream into the brain, overcoming the challenges posed by the BBB. The BBB is a highly selective and semi-permeable membrane that separates the circulating blood from the brain extracellular fluid, limiting the passage of molecules, including drugs, into the brain.</p><p>Efficient drug delivery across the BBB is crucial for treating various neurological disorders, including brain tumors, neurodegenerative diseases (such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's), and psychiatric disorders. Several strategies have been developed to enhance drug delivery across the BBB, including the use of carrier-mediated transporters, receptor-mediated transcytosis, modification of drugs or carriers, and focused ultrasound techniques.</p><p>The global market for drug delivery across the blood-brain barrier is witnessing significant growth.
