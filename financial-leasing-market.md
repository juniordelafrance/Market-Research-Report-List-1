<p><p>Financial leasing is a type of financing where a company obtains the use of an asset by making regular lease payments, without owning the asset. The market types within financial leasing include Sale and Leaseback, where a company sells an asset and then leases it back from the buyer. Direct Leasing involves a lessor (financial institution or leasing company) leasing an asset directly to a lessee (company). Leveraged Lease involves a third-party lender who funds a significant portion of the asset's cost. Straight Lease and Modified Lease refer to lease contracts with fixed or flexible terms respectively. The Primary Lease market involves initial lease agreements, while the Secondary Lease market deals with the transfer of existing lease contracts.</p></p>
