<p><p>The commercial photo printer market can be categorized based on the DPI (dots per inch) resolution offered by printers. DPI refers to the density of dots a printer can produce per square inch, indicating the level of detail in the printed image. 300 DPI printers produce good quality prints suitable for casual use. 600 DPI printers offer finer details suitable for professional photography. 1200 DPI printers ensure high-quality prints ideal for precise image reproduction. 2400 DPI printers deliver extremely sharp and detailed prints suitable for professional photo studios, while 4800 DPI printers offer the highest level of detail and are suitable for specialized applications such as fine art printing.</p></p>
