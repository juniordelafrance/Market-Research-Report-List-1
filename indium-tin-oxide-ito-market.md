<p><p>Indium Tin Oxide (ITO) is a transparent conductive material widely used in electronic devices such as touchscreens and solar cells. </p><p>Low temperature vacuum deposition is a technique used to deposit a thin layer of ITO onto a substrate in a vacuum chamber. This method is suitable for small-scale production but is time-consuming.</p><p>Sputtering is another method used to deposit ITO wherein high-energy ions bombard a target material, causing atoms to be ejected and then deposited onto a substrate. It is a faster and more efficient process than vacuum deposition.</p><p>Electron beam evaporation is a technique where an electron beam selectively heats ITO material in a vacuum, causing it to evaporate and then condense onto a substrate to form a thin film. It is a highly precise method but requires costly equipment.</p></p>
