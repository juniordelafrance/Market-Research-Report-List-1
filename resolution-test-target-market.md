<p><p>Resolution test targets are used in imaging systems to evaluate and measure the resolving power or ability of a system to distinguish fine details. There are different types of resolution test targets: </p><p>1. Star Targets: They consist of star-shaped patterns arranged in a grid, with the ability to assess a system's capability to resolve high-frequency details.</p><p>2. Sinusoidal Targets: They comprise sinusoidal patterns that aid in measuring a system's resolving power at different spatial frequencies.</p><p>3. Variable Frequency Targets: These targets feature patterns with varying spatial frequencies, allowing for a comprehensive evaluation of the system's resolving power.</p><p>4. USAF Pattern Wheel Target: It is a popular target consisting of a series of alternating bars and spaces, where each group has a unique spatial frequency. It aids in determining the system's ability to resolve different sizes of details.</p><p>5. Resolving Power Chart: It is a target that displays a range of patterns, representing increasing spatial frequencies. It is used to evaluate and quantify the resolving power of an imaging system.</p><p>6. IEEE Target Market: This term pertains to a specific market segment identified by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers. In the context of resolution test targets, the IEEE target market refers to the customers and industries that utilize these targets to assess and improve the performance of their imaging systems.</p></p>
