<p><p>Point of Care Ultrasound (POCUS) is a medical imaging technology used in non-traditional settings for immediate diagnoses. Trolley-based POCUS systems are larger and require transportation, typically found in hospitals. They provide higher-quality images but are less portable. Handheld POCUS devices are smaller, portable, and can be used in various settings like ambulances or remote locations. However, they may have lower image quality. Both types of POCUS systems enable real-time imaging, aiding healthcare professionals in making quicker and more accurate diagnoses at the point of patient care.</p></p>
