<p><p>Mendelian Disorders Testing is a type of genetic testing that is used to diagnose and identify genetic disorders that follow Mendelian inheritance patterns. These disorders are caused by mutations in a single gene and are usually inherited from one or both parents.</p><p>The Mendelian Disorders Testing Market is experiencing steady growth and is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.1% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. First, there is an increasing prevalence of genetic disorders worldwide, driving the demand for accurate and early diagnosis. Additionally, advancements in technology have made genetic testing more accessible and affordable, leading to a larger customer base. Moreover, the rising importance of personalized medicine and the growing awareness about the benefits of genetic testing are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the Mendelian Disorders Testing Market is the shift towards next-generation sequencing (NGS) techniques. NGS allows for the simultaneous testing of multiple genes, providing faster and more comprehensive results compared to traditional methods. This technology is particularly useful in identifying rare genetic disorders that may have been missed with previous testing methods. Additionally, the advent of NGS has led to the development of gene panels and exome sequencing, which are used to analyze specific sets of genes or the entire coding region of the genome, respectively.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the growing focus on non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT) for Mendelian disorders. NIPT involves analyzing fetal DNA present in the mother's blood, eliminating the need for invasive procedures such as amniocentesis or chorionic villus sampling. This approach provides a safer and more convenient option for genetic testing during pregnancy.</p><p>In conclusion, the Mendelian Disorders Testing Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing prevalence of genetic disorders, advances in technology, and the adoption of personalized medicine. The shift towards NGS techniques and the focus on non-invasive prenatal testing are some of the latest trends shaping the market.</p></p>

Predictive and pre-symptomatic testing assesses the risk of developing a disorder before symptoms appear. Prenatal testing is employed during pregnancy to detect genetic abnormalities in the fetus.
