<p><p>Inductive displacement sensors are used to measure the distance between an object and a sensor without physical contact. There are three main types of inductive displacement sensors in the market. The first type is the self inductive type, which uses a coil to generate a magnetic field and measures the change in self-inductance caused by the proximity of an object. The second type is the mutual inductive type, which measures the change in mutual inductance between two coils caused by an object's presence. The last type is the eddy current type, which measures the change in eddy currents induced in a conductive target by a separate coil. These sensors find applications in various industries for precise position and displacement measurements.</p></p>
