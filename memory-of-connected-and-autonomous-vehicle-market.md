<p><p>In the connected and autonomous vehicle market, various types of memories play a crucial role. DRAM (Dynamic Random Access Memory) enables fast data access and retrieval, making it suitable for tasks requiring high-speed processing. NAND flash memory offers high storage capacity and non-volatility, making it ideal for long-term data storage. SRAM (Static Random Access Memory) provides faster data access than DRAM and is often used for caching and buffering. Other memories, such as EEPROM (Electrically Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory), play a role in storing critical data or program code that needs to be retained even when power is lost.</p></p>
