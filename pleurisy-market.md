<p><p>Pleurisy is an inflammation of the pleura, the double-layered membrane surrounding the lungs. Antibiotics are medications that fight bacterial infections, and in the case of pleurisy caused by bacterial infection, they may be prescribed. Antifungals, on the other hand, specifically target fungal infections and are used when pleurisy is caused by a fungal infection. Blood thinners, or anticoagulants, are prescribed if blood clots are contributing to the development of pleurisy, as they help prevent clot formation and promote blood flow. These medications aid in treating specific causes of pleurisy, targeting the underlying infection or clotting issue.</p></p>
