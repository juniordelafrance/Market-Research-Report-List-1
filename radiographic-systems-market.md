<p><p>Radiographic systems refer to imaging systems used in medical and non-medical settings to capture and analyze internal structures of the body. The market for radiographic systems can be categorized into three types: analog, digital, and others. Analog systems use traditional film-based techniques to capture and develop X-ray images. Digital systems, on the other hand, convert X-rays into digital images for immediate viewing and analysis. Other systems include computed radiography, which uses imaging plates, and fluoroscopy, which provides real-time moving images. Each system has its own advantages and disadvantages, catering to different needs and preferences in the healthcare industry.</p></p>
