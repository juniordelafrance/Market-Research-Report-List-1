<p><p>The biomass dryer market primarily consists of two types: semi-automatic and fully automatic. </p><p>In semi-automatic biomass dryers, the drying process requires some manual intervention. Operators need to load the biomass material into the dryer, monitor the drying process, and unload the dried product. Semi-automatic dryers are typically less expensive and require less complex technology, making them popular among small-scale biomass producers. </p><p>On the other hand, fully automatic biomass dryers require minimal manual intervention. They employ advanced technology and automation systems to control the drying process from start to finish. Fully automatic dryers offer higher efficiency, more precise control over drying parameters, and are commonly used by large-scale biomass producers who prioritize productivity and operational efficiency.</p></p>
