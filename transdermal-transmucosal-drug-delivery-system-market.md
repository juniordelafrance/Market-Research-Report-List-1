<p><p>Transdermal Drug Delivery System is a type of drug delivery method that allows the administration of medication through the skin, bypassing the oral route. It involves the use of patches or creams applied to the skin, which slowly release the medication into the bloodstream. On the other hand, Transmucosal Drug Delivery System refers to the delivery of drugs through the mucous membranes, such as the nose, throat, or rectum. This method allows for faster absorption and avoids the first-pass metabolism by the liver. Both systems offer effective alternatives to traditional oral medication.</p></p>
