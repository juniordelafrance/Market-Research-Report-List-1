<p><p>Low Voltage Electron Microscopes (LVEM) are advanced imaging devices used for magnifying small objects at very high resolutions. LVEM market types include Scanning Electron Microscopes (SEM) and Transmission Electron Microscopes (TEM). SEM uses a focused electron beam to scan the surface of a sample, producing detailed images in 3D. TEM, on the other hand, passes an electron beam through a thin section of the sample to create a detailed cross-sectional image. Both SEM and TEM play crucial roles in various industries such as materials science, nanotechnology, and biological research.</p></p>
