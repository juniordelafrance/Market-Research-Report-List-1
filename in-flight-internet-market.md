<p><p>In-flight internet market encompasses two main types: Ground-based broadband and Satellite broadband. Ground-based broadband uses terrestrial cellular networks to provide internet connectivity to aircraft, offering faster speeds and lower latency. On the other hand, Satellite broadband relies on communication satellites to deliver internet access to planes, offering wider coverage but with potential issues related to latency and bandwidth. Both markets cater to the growing demand for connectivity during flights, providing passengers with the ability to stay connected and productive while in the air.</p></p>
