<p><p>Insulin Delivery Systems refer to the various methods of administering insulin to individuals with diabetes. The three main types of insulin delivery systems are Insulin Pens, Insulin Pumps, and Insulin Syringes. Insulin Pens are handheld devices that allow for convenient and precise insulin dosing through a disposable needle. Insulin Pumps, on the other hand, are worn on the body and deliver insulin continuously throughout the day via a small catheter. Insulin Syringes, the traditional method, involve manually drawing insulin from a vial into a syringe for injection. These systems provide different options for individuals to effectively manage their diabetes.</p></p>
