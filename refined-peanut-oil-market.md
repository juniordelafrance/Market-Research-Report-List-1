<p><p>Refined peanut oil comes in two main market types: cold pressed and hot pressed. Cold pressing involves extracting oil from peanuts using hydraulic pressure, maintaining low temperatures to preserve the oil's flavor and nutritional value. This method is favored by health-conscious consumers seeking natural products. In contrast, hot pressing involves applying heat during the extraction process, resulting in a higher oil yield but potentially diminishing the oil's quality and taste. Both types cater to different consumer preferences for oil production methods, offering choices for various culinary and dietary needs.</p></p>
